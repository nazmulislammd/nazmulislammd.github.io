Research Projects

Current Research Projects

I am passionate about applying advanced epidemiological and statistical tools to tackle zoonotic and infectious diseases at the human–animal–environment interface, with a focus on dairy cattle health in low-resource settings. My work integrates field diagnostics, Bayesian modeling, and One Health principles to generate actionable insights for disease control, antimicrobial stewardship, and sustainable livestock production. As an early-career researcher from Bangladesh Agricultural University, I am eager to collaborate with international teams to scale these approaches globally.

1. Epidemiological Study of Infectious Diseases in Large Dairy Cattle Herds in Bangladesh

Nov 2023 – Present

This ongoing project quantifies the true burden and transmission dynamics of priority zoonotic pathogens in intensive dairy systems — a critical yet understudied context in South Asia. Focus pathogens include brucellosis, paratuberculosis (Johne’s disease), bovine viral diarrhea (BVD), neosporosis, and Q fever (Coxiella burnetii). We use bulk milk screening and molecular diagnostics to address diagnostic imperfections and surveillance gaps.

Key contributions and outputs: - Estimated herd- and cow-level true prevalence using indirect ELISA and real-time PCR on bulk milk samples. - Identified farm-level risk factors (management practices, biosecurity, environmental exposures) via multivariable and Bayesian approaches. - Performed multiplex qPCR on fetal membranes from abortion cases to detect Brucella spp., Coxiella burnetii, Neospora caninum, Leptospira spp., and others. - Presented findings at the BSVER Annual Scientific Conference (2024). - Contributing to multiple under-review manuscripts in high-impact journals (e.g., PLOS ONE, Veterinary Sciences).

This work bridges local impact (improved herd health for Bangladeshi smallholders) with global relevance (zoonotic risk in emerging dairy economies).

Skills & Methods: Indirect ELISA · Real-time qPCR · DNA extraction · Bayesian prevalence estimation · Risk factor analysis · Scientific visualization (R, BioRender, Adobe Illustrator) · Zoonotic disease surveillance · Dairy herd health monitoring

2. Evaluating Locally Formulated Total Mixed Rations (TMR) for Beef Cattle

Jan 2025 – Present
Supervisor: Prof. Dr. A.K.M. Anisur Rahman

This project develops cost-effective, nutritionally optimized TMR formulations using locally sourced feeds to enhance growth performance, health, and welfare in Bangladeshi beef cattle — addressing feed scarcity and sustainability in tropical smallholder systems.

Key contributions: - Assisted in designing and executing controlled feeding trials with on-farm monitoring. - Applied laboratory diagnostics (ELISA, PCR) and statistical modeling (R) to evaluate impacts on growth metrics, immune response, and disease resistance. - Collaborated on practical recommendations for farmers, integrating veterinary medicine with food security and rural livelihoods.

This initiative demonstrates scalable solutions for climate-resilient livestock production in resource-limited regions.

Skills & Methods: Veterinary nutrition · Experimental trial design · Statistical analysis (R) · Laboratory techniques (ELISA, PCR) · Field data collection · One Health applications in livestock production

3. Global Epidemiology of Human Brucellosis: Bayesian Test-Adjusted Evidence Synthesis, Spatiotemporal Mapping, and One Health Driver Modeling

Self-initiated research program (leading a team of 10 collaborators)

This program addresses critical gaps in the global understanding of human brucellosis — a neglected zoonosis with massive underestimation due to imperfect diagnostics, heterogeneous surveillance, and non-specific symptoms. It comprises three interconnected, independently publishable studies:

  1. Bayesian test-adjusted meta-analysis — Synthesizing global seroprevalence while propagating uncertainty in diagnostic sensitivity/specificity (no perfect gold standard) to derive credible true prevalence estimates across regions.
  2. Bayesian spatiotemporal disease mapping — Inferring global hotspots, trends, and clusters under incomplete reporting, borrowing strength across space/time to overcome data sparsity.
  3. One Health ecological driver modeling — Quantifying environmental (climate, humidity), livestock density, and socioeconomic drivers while addressing spatial confounding and ecological inference biases.

This builds on my expertise in Bayesian latent-class models and zoonotic epidemiology, with strong potential for high-impact outputs and international collaboration (diagnostics, spatial epi, One Health groups).

Skills & Methods: Systematic review · Bayesian hierarchical modeling (latent class, spatiotemporal, ecological inference) · Advanced R programming · Geospatial analysis (QGIS) · One Health integration · Uncertainty propagation in diagnostics
